MTL Expressions

ID 1: Maximum and minimum speed limits.
  • Proposition ID p1: Maximum speed is 120 kilometers per hour.
    • Granularity: hours
  • Proposition ID p2: Minimum speed is 60 kilometers per hour.
    • Granularity: hours
  • MTL Expression: G(['0', '+inf']) (max_speed ∧ min_speed)
ID 2: Rules based on lane numbers for speeds.
  • Proposition ID p3: For two lanes, minimum speed of left lane is 100 kilometers per hour.
    • Assumption: Assuming only one vehicle is in the left lane at a time.
    • Granularity: hours
  • Proposition ID p4: For three or more lanes, minimum speed of farthest left lane is 110 kilometers per hour and 90 kilometers per hour for the middle lane.
    • Assumption: Assuming only one vehicle is in each lane at a time.
    • Ambiguity: Assuming that when three or more lanes, 'middle lane' means the lane to the right of the leftmost lane, not the geometric center.
    • Granularity: hours
  • MTL Expression: G(['0', '+inf']) ((two_lanes ∧ min_speed_left_lane) ∨ (three_or_more_lanes ∧ left_lane_and_middle_lane_speed))
ID 3: Case of a speed limit sign with a different speed.
  • Proposition ID p5: Where there is any discrepancy between the speed indicated by a speed limit sign put up on a road and the driving speeds mentioned above, a motor vehicle shall be driven at the speed indicated by the speed limit sign.
    • Assumption: Assuming that vehicles can detect speed limit signs and they are always accurate.
    • Granularity: seconds
  • MTL Expression: G(['0', '+inf']) (speed_limit_sign_affects ∨ ¬speed_limit_sign_affects)

Dependency Sequence

From ID 1 to ID 2: The rules based on lane numbers for speeds operate within the universal maximum and minimum speed limits.
From ID 2 to ID 3: The presence of a speed limit sign with a different speed can override the pre-existing speed rules.
